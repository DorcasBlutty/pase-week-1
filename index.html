Climate Change And Gender.

by Dorcas Blutty.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change describes a change in the average weather condition such as temperature, pricipitation and humidityof a particular geographical area over a long period of time like 30 years.

Climate change has a greater impact on countries that are most reliant on natural resources and/or who have the least capacity to repond tonatural hazards such as floods and droughts. Women commonly face higher risks and greater burdens from the impacts in situations of poverty and majority of the world's poor are women Women's unequal participation in the decision making process and labour markets compound inequalities and often prevent women from fully contributing to climate related planning, policy-making and implementation.
